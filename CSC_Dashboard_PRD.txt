1. OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The CSC Dashboard consolidates fragmented toolbox planning, asset management,
transcreation workflows, and market approvals into a single pane of glass —
eliminating manual file transfers, broken handoffs, and opaque status tracking
across the content supply chain.

The Content Supply Chain currently operates across 4+ disconnected systems with
manual handoffs at every stage. A unified dashboard that mirrors the end-to-end
workflow — from toolbox planning through transcreation to market activation —
will reduce cycle time, eliminate file-transfer errors, and give every
stakeholder the situational awareness they need to act without waiting.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. PROBLEM STATEMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The following pain points were identified by analysing the As-Is processes
across all stakeholder roles.

[CRITICAL] Invisible Pipeline
  No stakeholder has a real-time view of where a toolbox is in the end-to-end
  journey — from agency briefing through master asset creation, transcreation,
  and market go-live.

[CRITICAL] Manual Asset Logistics
  Content Delivery & Transcreation Teams manually download, rename, transfer,
  and re-upload files. This introduces errors, delays, and version drift at
  every handoff.

[HIGH] Siloed Tooling
  Brand Portal, Workfront, StudioOne, and Brand Central are disconnected. Users
  context-switch between 4+ tools, duplicating effort and losing traceability.

[HIGH] Market Exclusion
  Market Users cannot self-serve assets or track CDT localisation progress.
  They raise PowerPoint briefs and wait, with no ETA visibility.

[MEDIUM] Risk Blindness
  Programme managers lack a consolidated view of at-risk milestones across
  simultaneous toolbox programmes (e.g. Veev One, TEREA Remaster running
  in parallel).

3. USERS & PERSONAS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

TOOLBOX OWNER
  Responsible for: Planning scope, audience, and budget. Coordinating creative,
  production, and channel agencies.

  As-Is Pain Points:
    - No single source of truth for programme status
    - Manual playbook creation and publishing

  To-Be Experience:
    - Creates projects via Brand Central using the global playbook template
    - Manages plans and risks in Workfront
    - Publishes digital playbook to Brand Central


CREATIVE AGENCY
  Responsible for: Creating toolbox content — concepts, master visuals, and
  channel-ready assets.

  As-Is Pain Points:
    - Brief received via PowerPoint, no structured format
    - Approval loops are email-based

  To-Be Experience:
    - Receives structured brief in Workfront
    - Submits concepts for inline approval
    - Uploads approved master assets directly to Brand Central


PRODUCTION TEAM
  Responsible for: Managing Production Agency. Overseeing asset adaptation
  and quality.

  As-Is Pain Points:
    - Task management fragmented across Workfront and email
    - Version control issues on master files

  To-Be Experience:
    - Full task visibility in Workfront
    - Asset versioning tracked in Brand Central


CONTENT DELIVERY & TRANSCREATION TEAM
  Responsible for: Adapting toolbox content for local markets; ensuring brand
  and legal compliance.

  As-Is Pain Points:
    - Manually downloads, transfers, and uploads files
    - Struggles to find and manage source assets

  To-Be Experience:
    - Receives requests via Workfront with attached files
    - Works in shared environment (no download needed)
    - Marks tasks complete and triggers market review automatically


CHANNEL AGENCY
  Responsible for: Creating channel assets referencing master files. Updating
  digital playbook.

  As-Is Pain Points:
    - No direct access to master assets until email confirmation
    - Playbook updates are manual

  To-Be Experience:
    - Self-serve asset access in Brand Central
    - Playbook updates automated on asset approval


MARKET USER
  Responsible for: Planning local campaigns, requesting CDT changes, and
  approving localised content.

  As-Is Pain Points:
    - Manually searches for completed files
    - No visibility on CDT localisation progress

  To-Be Experience:
    - Browse and build shopping basket requests in Brand Central
    - Review and approve CDT localised content
    - Access completed files directly from Brand Central library

5. FEATURES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

F1 | PROGRAMME COMMAND CENTRE [P0]
  The top-level view of all active toolbox programmes. Gantt + card view showing
  milestones, owners, and RAG status.

  User Stories:
    01. As a Toolbox Owner, I can see all active programmes in a single dashboard
        with current phase and health status.
    02. As a Programme Manager, I can filter by toolbox, phase, owner, and
        at-risk flag.
    03. As any stakeholder, I can drill into a programme to see the full task
        timeline.
    04. Milestones from the CSC Plan CSV auto-sync into the dashboard view.


F2 | DIGITISED PLAYBOOK BUILDER [P0]
  Structured digital playbook creation from a global template, replacing manual
  PowerPoint/Brand Portal process.

  User Stories:
    01. As a Toolbox Owner, I can create a new playbook project using the global
        Brand Central template.
    02. Playbook auto-populates with approved master assets upon milestone
        completion.
    03. Publish to Brand Central with one click — no manual upload or version
        management.
    04. Version history and diff view for all playbook iterations.


F3 | ASSET HUB & SHOPPING BASKET [P0]
  Centralised asset browsing, filtering, and request mechanism for Market Users
  and Channel Agencies.

  User Stories:
    01. As a Market User, I can browse and filter assets by market, channel,
        format, and language.
    02. As a Market User, I can build a shopping basket of assets and submit a
        CDT request in-platform.
    03. Asset availability is gated by approval status — no pre-approval assets
        visible to markets.
    04. Search filters include: asset type, toolbox, market, date range,
        approval status.


F4 | TRANSCREATION BRIEF & WORKFLOW [P0]
  End-to-end transcreation request flow — brief creation, file attachment, CDT
  work, market review, and approval — without leaving the platform.

  User Stories:
    01. Brief auto-generated with source files attached when Market User submits
        basket request.
    02. CDT receives brief in Workfront with all files pre-linked (no manual
        download).
    03. CDT marks task complete → auto-notifies Market User for review.
    04. Market User approves/rejects with inline annotation; rejection triggers
        CDT rework task.
    05. Completed localised assets auto-land in Market User's Brand Central
        library.


F5 | RISK & MILESTONE TRACKER [P1]
  Proactive risk surfacing across all concurrent programmes, derived from
  CSC Plan milestone data.

  User Stories:
    01. At-risk tasks flagged automatically when completion probability drops
        based on predecessor delays.
    02. Weekly risk digest email sent to programme owners.
    03. RAG (Red/Amber/Green) status visible on all programme cards.
    04. Milestone dependency visualisation (predecessor relationships from
        CSV plan).


F6 | ANALYTICS & REPORTING [P1]
  Data point tracking for Brand Central usage, CDT throughput, and market
  adoption metrics.

  User Stories:
    01. Dashboard with programme velocity, brief volume, and CDT cycle time.
    02. Market-level adoption reporting: % of assets accessed vs. available.
    03. Export reports as CSV or PDF for stakeholder presentations.
    04. Brand Central Data Point Tracking feeds automatically into analytics
        layer.


F7 | NOTIFICATION & INTEGRATION LAYER [P2]
  Bidirectional sync with Workfront and Brand Central; in-app and email
  notifications for key events.

  User Stories:
    01. Workfront task status changes reflected in dashboard within 5 minutes.
    02. Users notified when their tasks are unblocked or approaching SLA.
    03. Integration with StudioOne for creative agency brief handoff.
    04. Audit log of all status changes, approvals, and file uploads.

6. USER FLOWS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

TOOLBOX OWNER — NEW PROGRAMME LAUNCH
  1. Create project in Brand Central using global playbook template
  2. Define scope, budget, market targets, and key milestones
  3. Build shopping basket with custom elements (channels, formats, markets)
  4. Assign tasks to Creative Agency via Workfront integration
  5. Monitor programme health on Command Centre dashboard
  6. Publish approved playbook to Brand Central


MARKET USER — REQUEST LOCALISED ASSET
  1. Browse Asset Hub — filter by market, channel, format
  2. Add required assets to shopping basket
  3. Submit CDT request with localisation brief (in-platform, no PowerPoint)
  4. Track CDT progress via brief status tracker
  5. Receive notification when localised assets are ready for review
  6. Approve or reject with annotation → assets land in library on approval


CDT TEAM — TRANSCREATION WORKFLOW
  1. Receive brief notification in Workfront (files pre-attached)
  2. Open shared asset environment — no download needed
  3. Complete transcreation work in-environment
  4. Run brand & legal compliance checklist
  5. Mark task complete → market user notified automatically
  6. Handle any market rejection → rework task auto-created

8. NON-GOALS (OUT OF SCOPE FOR V1)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - Not a creative production tool: The dashboard orchestrates workflow but does
    not replace creative software (Photoshop, InDesign, After Effects).

  - Not a media buying platform: Campaign activation and paid media scheduling
    are out of scope.

  - Not a global DAM replacement: Brand Central remains the system of record for
    assets; this dashboard surfaces and routes assets, not stores them.

  - No v1 mobile app: Initial release is desktop web only. Mobile is Phase 2.

  - No real-time co-editing: Collaborative real-time document editing
    (Google Docs-style) is not in scope for v1.
